A single borrower closed a VA purchase loan with Rocket Mortgage on January 7, 2025. What followed was 49 solicitations from 24 different companies in the first 10 months — most designed to imply VA program affiliation, government authority, or a direct relationship with the borrower's actual lender.

Data collected from direct mail received by a single veteran borrower following a January 7, 2025 VA purchase loan closing with Rocket Mortgage in Summit County, Ohio. All companies shown are third-party solicitors with no affiliation to the VA, Rocket Mortgage, or the U.S. government. Borrower information was sourced entirely from public county deed and mortgage recording records — a standard industry practice. Click “View PDF” on any card to see the original mailer.

How did they know?
When a mortgage closes, the deed and loan details are recorded in public county records — a legal requirement in every U.S. state. Data aggregators scrape these records continuously and sell the lists to mortgage solicitors within weeks of closing. Every company in this timeline knew the borrower's name, address, loan type, lender, and approximate loan amount — entirely from public records, with no access to the actual loan file. The loan closed in Summit County, Ohio.

Interesting findings
01
Unprecedented document insert
Aven included a "Sample Certificate of Lien" — a ornate, certificate-styled insert complete with guilloche border and official seal — to normalize the concept of placing a lien on the borrower's home as part of a credit card product. Nothing else in the dataset comes close to this as a document design tactic.
02
Strongest false affiliation claim in the dataset
Denali Funding Group includes standard non-affiliation language in the letter body — but buried in the fine print disclosure is the statement that the offer is made by a company "partnered with Rocket Mortgage." No other mailer in the dataset attempts this. Every other sender disclaimed any connection to the borrower's lender; Denali explicitly asserted one in the same document. The contradiction between the body copy and the fine print is what makes it notable: the disclaimer is present, and the false affiliation claim is also present.
03
Three coordinated print operations confirmed
Four fulfillment operations have been identified producing mailers in this dataset. Sacramento CA Permit #1946 served MVP Funding, AdvantageFirst, Prosperity, Independence, and Shield Home Loans. Bellmawr NJ Permit #2139 served United Trust Bank and ClearPath Lending. ZIP 30336 in Atlanta served Denali Funding Group, Equity Express Mortgage, AdvantageFirst, and Everett Financial Group — the Atlanta operation is also responsible for the "DATED DOCUMENT VERIFICATION REQUESTED" template used by at least two different companies. A fourth operation at Temecula CA ZIP 92591 Permit No. 2 served AdvantageFirst, Liberty 1 Lending, and Vesta Lending. Across all four, at least 14 of the 49 mailers were produced by shared fulfillment infrastructure serving multiple competing lenders simultaneously.
04
USPS impersonation envelope used by three separate companies
The eagle + "UNITED STATES MAIL" envelope design — which mimics official USPS postage markings — was used by Prime Choice Funding (Permit #1935), Active Link (Permit #681), and a third sender. Active Link escalated further by printing "CONFIDENTIAL MATERIALS ENCLOSED" and "Re: Rocket Mortgage LLC" on the exterior of the envelope — visible before the borrower even opened it.
05
The check format — used by two companies
United Trust Bank formatted their solicitation as a physical check — written-out dollar amount ("TWO HUNDRED FIFTY-SEVEN THOUSAND..."), printed VP signature, perforated edge, and a "VA WAITING PERIOD: COMPLETED" stamp. MVP Funding used a similar check-style insert for a smaller fabricated amount ($2,493). Both included "THIS IS NOT A LIVE CHECK" or equivalent — in small print, after the entire design had already done its work.
06
Prosperity Home Mortgage fabricated VA form numbers
Prosperity's August 1 mailing cited "IRRRL-1003 Benefit Allotment" and "Form 21-0760 Eligibility Notification" — neither of which are real VA forms. The actual relevant VA form is 26-0592. The document also instructed the borrower to "PLEASE KEEP A COPY OF THIS NOTICE FOR YOUR RECORDS" — standard government document language applied to a private solicitation.
07
SoFi was the least deceptive sender — by a wide margin
SoFi sent four mailers across the dataset — all using clean, branded fintech design with no government impersonation, no fabricated urgency, and no lender-naming tactics. All four came from the same Carol Stream IL Permit #35. They stand in stark contrast to the rest of the dataset and demonstrate that compliant, straightforward solicitation is entirely possible in this space.
08
Federal mail tampering statute on a private solicitation
AdvantageFirst Lending's September 25 mailing printed the full text of 18 U.S.C. § 1702 — the federal mail obstruction law, carrying five years imprisonment — on the face of the letter, alongside "DATED DOCUMENT VERIFICATION REQUESTED" and "CONFIDENTIAL DOCUMENT FOR ADDRESSEE ONLY." The Atlanta ZIP 30336 operation produced the same "DATED DOCUMENT VERIFICATION" template for Everett Financial Group the following month, confirming this is a template sold to multiple clients.
09
Four coordinated print operations identified — not three
A fourth fulfillment operation was confirmed: Temecula CA ZIP 92591, Permit No. 2, producing mailings for AdvantageFirst Lending, Liberty 1 Lending, and Vesta Lending. The Atlanta 30336 operation also expanded — it produced the "DATED DOCUMENT VERIFICATION" template for both AdvantageFirst and Everett Financial Group. The Sacramento CA Permit #1946 operation added Shield Home Loans to its client roster. Across all four operations, at least 14 of the 47 mailers were produced by shared fulfillment infrastructure serving multiple competing lenders.
